Why Morocco?

Morocco, the gateway to Africa

Morocco, known as Al Maghrib in Arabic, stands as a vibrant crossroads where African, Arab, and European cultures blend seamlessly. With a history spanning millennia, Morocco has been shaped by Berber tribes, Arab conquests, and European influences, especially French and Spanish. This rich tapestry of cultures has created a nation unlike any other in North Africa.

Living in Morocco for seven months and delivering my baby in Gueliz taught me to appreciate the country's remarkable contrasts. Beyond the bustling souks filled with artisans crafting intricate wares, tranquil riads offering respite from the desert heat, and world-class restaurants serving authentic Moroccan cuisine, you can still wander the ancient medinas and feel centuries of history beneath your feet, taking in the terracotta walls, ornate tilework, and on the horizon, the majestic Atlas Mountains capped with snow.

RIADS

My favorite riads in Morocco

Riad BE Marrakech

A peaceful oasis in the heart of the Medina with a stunning rooftop terrace and refreshing plunge pool.

Address:

Derb Sidi Ahmed Ben Nasser 34, Medina

What I like about it

The perfect blend of traditional Moroccan architecture with modern comforts. The staff treats you like family, and the breakfast is an absolute feast!

Le Riad Yasmine

Instagram-famous riad with its iconic green-tiled pool surrounded by lush plants and traditional decor.

Address:

209 Rue Ank Jemel, Bab Taghzout, Medina

What I like about it

The interior courtyard feels like a secret garden. A peaceful sanctuary after exploring the bustling souks all day.

Riad Fes

Luxury riad in Fes featuring stunning Andalusian architecture, multiple courtyards, and a world-class spa.

Address:

5 Derb Ben Slimane Zerbtana, Fes

What I like about it

The attention to historical detail is breathtaking. The rooftop offers panoramic views of the entire medina of Fes, especially magical at sunset.

FOOD

Must-try Moroccan dishes

Tagine

Slow-cooked stew named after the distinctive earthenware pot it's cooked in, with countless regional variations.

Where to try it:

Al Fassia Restaurant, Marrakech

Why I love it

The lamb with prunes and almonds tagine changed my understanding of how sweet and savory flavors can harmonize. The meat becomes incredibly tender from the slow cooking process.

Pastilla

A sweet-savory pie with layers of paper-thin pastry, traditionally filled with pigeon, but often made with chicken today.

Where to try it:

Dar Hatim, Fes

Why I love it

The contrast between the powdered sugar and cinnamon topping with the savory spiced meat filling is unexpected and delightful. It's a perfect representation of Moroccan cuisine's complexity.

Harira

A hearty tomato-based soup with lentils, chickpeas, and tender pieces of lamb, traditionally eaten to break the fast during Ramadan.

Where to try it:

Café Clock, Fes

Why I love it

This comforting soup got me through my pregnancy! The blend of fresh herbs, spices, and hearty ingredients makes it the perfect meal in itself, especially with a side of dates.

EXPERIENCES

Unforgettable Moroccan moments

Traditional Hammam

An authentic Moroccan bathing ritual that involves steam, black soap scrubs, and relaxing massage.

Where to experience it:

Hammam de la Rose, Marrakech

Why it's special

The hammam ritual dates back centuries and remains an important part of Moroccan culture. You'll leave feeling completely renewed—I went every week during my pregnancy!

Sahara Desert Trek

Camel trek through the golden dunes of Erg Chebbi, followed by a night in a Berber camp under countless stars.

Where to experience it:

Merzouga

Why it's special

The silence of the desert is profound. Watching the sunrise over the dunes while sipping mint tea is a moment of pure magic that will stay with you forever.

The Blue City

Exploring the enchanting blue-washed streets of Chefchaouen nestled in the Rif Mountains.

Where to experience it:

Chefchaouen Medina

Why it's special

Walking through Chefchaouen feels like stepping into another world. The calming blue palette, combined with the friendliness of locals, makes it the perfect place to slow down and simply be.
